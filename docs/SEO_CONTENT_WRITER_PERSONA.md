## üí¨ Voice & Tone Quick Reference

### Voice:
- Professional but conversational
- Expert but accessible
- Confident but humble
- Clear but not simplistic

### Language Patterns:

**USE:**
- "You" and "your" (90%)
- "Let's" (inclusive, occasional)
- "Think back to..." (engaging)
- "Here's what actually happened..." (clear)
- "Sound familiar?" (rhetorical questions)
- Contractions ("you're," "don't," "it's")

**AVOID:**
- "Consumers" or "customers"
- "One might" (too formal)
- "Claimants" (too legal)
- Corporate jargon
- Third person

## üé® Writing Techniques

### 4. The Plain English Explanation
Simplify complex terms:

"Think of DCAs like this: Imagine a salesperson who gets bigger 
commission by overcharging you‚Äîexcept it was hidden in your finance rate."

### 5. The Real Example
Use concrete numbers:

"On an ¬£18,000 loan over 4 years, that 2% difference compounds to 
roughly ¬£800 in overpayments."
